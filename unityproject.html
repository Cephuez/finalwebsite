Unity Project Link

For most of my spare time, I worked on my own videogame designs. I worked with Unity Engine since it provides everything for me to start a game. For my game projects, I created my own assets like sprite animations, sounds, and code. I coded the physics of the game, character movement, animation, sound design, AI controls, and others. Even though my game projects are unfinished, I can demonstrate how it looks like through gifs. I have linked my coding scripts to my github page. You can click the button above to read them. They were all coded in C#.

I would like to demonstrate my work through my Mysterious Forest prototype game. As you can see, I code the running , dashing, and climbing animations. You can see two unique enemies that have a different pathing. Yellow monster walks left and right, while tortoise tries to chase the player. I also provide a inventory menu where the player could change weapons and items.

Furthermore, I demonstrate more different enemies. A bear that waits before charging at the player, and a monkey that jumps to the location of the player.

The next enemies is a plant that shoots a spike at the player's position, a plant that uses whips to attack at the player, and a living tree that moves once the player is near them.

The last one is a bird that chargest its flight power before charging at the player. The bird continues to charge at the player until they are out of sight.

My games were a prototype that allowed me to play with a lot of Unity's features. I played with different AI coding by making each enemy have a unique pathing to attack the player. You are free to view my code under every .c files in my Unity Project folder over github. You can click the button at the top to navigate to it.
